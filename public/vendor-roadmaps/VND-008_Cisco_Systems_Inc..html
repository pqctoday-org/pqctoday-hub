Post-Quantum Cryptography

Quantum-Safe Computing

As quantum computing advances and estimated timelines evolve, the time to prepare is now. Discover how Cisco’s two-pillar strategy helps secure your communications and hardware while supporting a measured transition to mitigate 'Harvest Now, Decrypt Later' risks.

The Quantum Threat: Why Action is Needed Now

We are entering a new era of digital trust where the encryption securing our world today must evolve to withstand the quantum computers of tomorrow. Two leading threats are at the forefront:

  • Harvest Now, Decrypt Later (HNDL): Attackers are capturing encrypted data now, aiming to decrypt it later when quantum computers are ready.
  • Trust Now, Forge Later (TNFL): Digital signatures that are trusted and verified today could be retroactively forged once quantum computers become powerful enough

To protect the integrity of global communications, Cisco is advancing a practical transition to Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC). We are embedding quantum-safe resilience into the very fabric of our products to ensure your data remains secure for the long term.

Blog

Our Strategic Approach

Cisco delivers end-to-end quantum resilience from the silicon to the cloud:

  • Secure Communications: Integrating PQC into core protocols (IPsec, TLS, MACsec) to protect data in transit. 

  • Secure Products: Embedding quantum-resistant trust foundations into Cisco platforms, anchored in a hardware root of trust that protects key material and enables PQC-based identity, secure boot, signing and verification, update integrity, and remote attestation – so customers can continuously validate device authenticity and software integrity.

Getting Started on Your Post-Quantum Cryptography Journey

Preparing for quantum safety starts with understanding where quantum risk exists today. Cisco helps organizations build awareness of the quantum threat, assess cryptographic exposure across systems and data flows, and prioritize sensitive data requiring long-term protection. This foundation supports informed planning for cryptographic agility, supplier readiness, and phased PQC adoption as standards mature, so customers can begin a structured, ongoing transition with clarity and confidence.
